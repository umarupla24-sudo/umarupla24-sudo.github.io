MP Doors Impact Hinged Patio Door (MasterPiece Series)

For homeowners in Florida, Texas, and coastal windstorm zones — the only composite patio door in the MP Doors line carrying both Florida Building Code certification and Texas Department of Insurance windstorm approval.

There is a category of patio door purchase where the buyer does not have a choice about performance specifications — the building code makes it for them. In Florida windstorm zones and Texas coastal regions, a patio door that does not carry the right certification does not pass inspection. The Impact Hinged Patio Door is the door that passes. FBC# FL-29714 for Florida Building Code compliance. TDI certification for Texas Department of Insurance windstorm approval. DP50 impact rating tested to ASTM E1886/E1996 standards — the same protocol applied to hurricane shutters. Why This Door

What the Certification Means in Practice

Five reasons Florida and Texas coastal buyers choose the Impact Hinged Patio Door — starting with the permit number.

Florida Building Code Certified — FBC# FL-29714

This door carries the specific Florida Building Code product number required for permit approval in Florida windstorm designation areas.

Which means: your contractor can pull the permit with this door's FBC number, and the inspection passes.

Texas Department of Insurance Certified

TDI windstorm certification for Texas coastal zones.

Which means: homeowners in Texas windstorm impact zones can install this door and maintain their windstorm insurance coverage.

DP50 Impact Rating — ASTM Tested

DP50 impact rating tested to ASTM E1886 and ASTM E1996 standards — the same test protocols applied to hurricane shutters and commercial storm protection systems.

Which means: the door's resistance to impact and pressure differential has been independently verified under the standards that matter in windstorm jurisdictions.

1-Inch Triple-Layer LoE Impact Glass

The glass unit is 1 inch thick, triple-layer, with LoE coating — engineered specifically for the impact load and thermal performance requirements of coastal applications.

Which means: the glass does not downgrade the performance of the composite frame — the entire door system is engineered for the same performance level.

Composite Frame — Coastal Durability

HydroShield Technology on all six sides. The composite frame resists salt air corrosion, humidity-related rot, UV degradation, warping, splitting, and delamination — the specific failure modes that coastal environments accelerate in traditional door materials.

Which means: in the climate where impact certification matters most — high humidity, salt air, UV exposure — the frame performs without the deterioration timeline that painted steel or wood frames run on in coastal conditions.

Built for Where You Live

Engineered for Coastal Conditions

The Impact Hinged Patio Door is engineered specifically for the climate conditions that require impact certification: coastal humidity, salt air, UV exposure, and the windstorm load conditions that Florida and Texas windstorm zones experience.

The composite frame addresses all of those conditions simultaneously — it does not corrode in salt air the way steel does, it does not rot in coastal humidity the way wood does, and it does not accumulate the surface deterioration that requires periodic refinishing in painted frame materials. The DP50 impact rating means the door holds through the windstorm events the building code is written for. The Energy Star certification in all four U.S. climate zones means it also meets thermal performance standards in the same climate where it has to meet impact standards.

Florida and Texas homeowners in windstorm designation zones should confirm their specific zone designation before selecting a door. The impact certification requirements vary by zone. Warranty & Support

What Is Covered

The frame, panels, and astragals carry a Limited Lifetime Warranty for the original purchaser — valid as long as you own your home. The impact glass seal is covered for 10 years prorated (shorter than the standard 20-year prorated coverage on non-impact glass, reflecting the specialized nature of the impact glass unit). The locking mechanism carries a 10-year warranty. Factory painted finish: 2 years. Labor is not covered. Installation by a licensed and bonded contractor is required for warranty coverage — a particularly important requirement in windstorm certification jurisdictions where the installation method is part of the certification record. Report delivery damage within 5 calendar days — call 1-888-366-7717.
