Certifications at a Glance

Built to Meet the Code — and Explain It

These certifications are not optional in windstorm zones. Here's what each one means for your permit, your insurance, and your home.

Florida

FBC# FL-29714

Required for permit approval in any Florida windstorm designation area. Your contractor uses this number to pull the permit — and the inspection passes.

Texas

TDI Windstorm Certified

Texas Department of Insurance certification for coastal zones. Required to maintain windstorm insurance coverage — install a non-certified door and your policy may not pay out after a storm.

Why This Door

What the Certification Means in Practice

Five reasons Florida and Texas coastal buyers choose the Impact Hinged Patio Door — starting with the permit number.

Florida Building Code Certified — FBC# FL-29714

This door carries the specific Florida Building Code product number required for permit approval in Florida windstorm designation areas.

Which means: your contractor can pull the permit with this door's FBC number, and the inspection passes.

Texas Department of Insurance Certified

TDI windstorm certification for Texas coastal zones.

Which means: homeowners in Texas windstorm impact zones can install this door and maintain their windstorm insurance coverage.

DP50 Impact Rating — ASTM Tested

DP50 impact rating tested to ASTM E1886 and ASTM E1996 standards — the same test protocols applied to hurricane shutters and commercial storm protection systems.

1-Inch Triple-Layer LoE Impact Glass

The glass unit is 1 inch thick, triple-layer, with LoE coating — engineered specifically for the impact load and thermal performance requirements of coastal applications.

Composite Frame — Coastal Durability

HydroShield Technology on all six sides. The composite frame resists salt air corrosion, humidity-related rot, UV degradation, warping, and splitting — the specific failure modes that coastal environments accelerate in traditional door materials. Verify Your Windstorm Zone Before You Buy

Florida and Texas homeowners in windstorm designation zones should confirm their specific zone designation before selecting a door. The impact certification requirements vary by zone. Your contractor or local building department can confirm which certification your specific location requires. FBC# FL-29714 covers Florida Building Code windstorm requirements. TDI certification covers Texas coastal windstorm zones.
