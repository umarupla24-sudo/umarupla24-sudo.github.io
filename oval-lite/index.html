Oval Lite Entry Door

MP Doors Oval Lite Entry Door

Enough glass to matter. Enough panel to mean something.

For homeowners who want refined elegance at the front entry — an architectural oval glass insert that brings diffused light into the entry without full glass exposure, in a composite frame engineered to hold its form and finish without maintenance.

There is a category of entry door design that sits between the full glass statement and the solid panel — where the glass is considered rather than maximized, and the door's character comes from proportion and restraint rather than glass area. The Oval Lite entry door is built for that design sensibility. The oval glass insert is soft where rectangular lites are angular, diffused where clear glass is exposed, and considered where full-lite designs are dominant.

Why This Door

Oval Glass — Soft, Considered Character

The oval glass insert creates a visual accent that reads as architectural design rather than standard catalogue selection — a shape distinct from the rectangular lites of Craftsman, colonial, and modern profiles.

Which means the front entry has a specific character that visitors recognize as a design choice, not a default.

Diffused Light — Without Interior Exposure

The oval glass brings natural light into the entry without the full interior visibility that a full-lite or large-panel glass door creates.

Which means the entry foyer gets light without the privacy trade-off that comes with a glass-dominant front door.

Two Configurations — 3/4 Oval and Prestige Oval

Available in two oval glass configurations — the 3/4 Oval Lite and the Prestige Oval Lite — for different proportional relationships between glass and panel area.

Which means the oval detailing can be selected in the configuration that best fits the scale of the door and the style of the home's exterior.

Composite Frame — Holds Finish Without Maintenance

The composite frame is 100% waterproof and does not rot, warp, split, delaminate, dent, or rust — and holds its painted or stained finish without a refinishing cycle.

Which means the design character of the Oval Lite entry holds in place in year eight without a contractor appointment to keep it there.

Energy Star — All Climate Zones

Energy Star certified in all four U.S. climate zones: Northern, North Central, South Central, and Southern.

Which means the front entry meets federal thermal efficiency standards whether the home is in a cold northern climate or a hot coastal environment.

Built for Where You Live

The oval glass insert is proportionally smaller than the glass area of a full-lite or modern full-lite door, which means the thermal performance of the entry door overall is less dependent on the glass unit than in glass-dominant designs. Energy Star certification in all four U.S. climate zones is held for the door system regardless.

The composite frame handles the full range of climate exposures — northern freeze-thaw cycles, southern humidity and UV exposure, and coastal salt air — without the deterioration timeline that wood frames run on in any of those climates. The finish the door ships with holds through years of weather exposure without periodic refinishing to keep it there.
