Full Lite Entry Door
MasterPiece Series

MP Doors Full Lite Entry Door

A full-glass front door that gives you light, presence, and — with the built-in blinds option — privacy you control with one hand.

For buyers who want a grand, glass-dominant front entry without the compromises of a decorative-glass door that cannot be made private — full glass area, composite construction that holds its form, and the option of built-in blinds between the glass.

There is a specific kind of front entry door that makes a home's exterior feel complete rather than utilitarian — and it almost always involves glass, lots of it. The Full Lite entry door maximizes the glass area of a standard single door, bringing natural light into the entry while making an unmistakable visual statement from the street. The built-in blinds option — factory-installed between the glass panels — answers the privacy question without adding a separate purchase, a cord on the door, or a blinds system that needs to be replaced when it wears out.

Why This Door

Maximum Glass Area — Full Lite Design

The full lite configuration fills the door panel with glass from near the top rail to near the bottom rail — the maximum glass area in a standard single-door format.

Which means the entry foyer receives the kind of natural light that a half-lite or three-quarter-lite door cannot deliver — and the exterior reads as architecturally open rather than closed.

Built-In Blinds Between the Glass — Optional

Select configurations include blinds factory-installed between the glass panes — operable from inside the home without exposing the blinds mechanism to room air, dust, or physical wear.

Which means on the days you want privacy at the front door, you close the blinds in one motion, without a cord to manage or a separate blind panel mounted on the glass.

Composite Frame — Holds Its Form Season to Season

The fiberglass door system features complete composite edging and full composite frame construction — 100% waterproof, engineered not to rot, warp, split, delaminate, dent, or rust.

Which means the frame that holds all that glass in place does not change dimension with the season — critical in a full-lite door, where any frame movement becomes dramatically visible across a full panel of glass.

Factory Pre-Hung — Precision from Day One

Arrives as a factory-assembled, pre-hung door system with factory-set tolerances between the door slab and the frame.

Which means the glass plane sits flat and the door swings true on installation day — a contractor installing a pre-hung unit is not making frame adjustments in the field that can introduce the slight twists and gaps that show in a glass-dominant door more than any other type.

Energy Star — All Climate Zones

Energy Star certified in all four U.S. climate zones — Northern, North Central, South Central, and Southern.

Which means the large glass area of a full-lite door meets federal thermal performance standards in every regional climate, not just temperate zones where glass-dominant entry doors are most common.

Built for Where You Live

A full-lite front entry door has a larger glass area than virtually any other door type — which makes the thermal performance of the glass unit more consequential than in a door with a smaller glass insert. Energy Star certification in all four U.S. climate zones means this door meets the thermal efficiency standard in cold northern climates as well as hot southern ones.

The composite frame is particularly relevant in climates with hard winters: unlike wood frames, which absorb moisture and swell against a full glass panel, composite frames maintain their dimension through freeze-thaw cycles. In humid coastal and southern climates, the composite frame does not accumulate the surface deterioration that requires periodic refinishing on a wood entry.

Warranty & Support

Frame, panels, and structural components carry a Limited Lifetime Warranty for the original purchaser. For standard (non-blinds) glass, the glass seal is covered for 20 years prorated. If your door includes built-in blinds between the glass, the glass seal warranty is 5 years prorated and the magnetic blind components are covered for 1 year. The locking mechanism carries a 10-year warranty. Factory painted finish: 2 years. Labor is not covered. Installation by a licensed and bonded contractor is required for warranty coverage.
