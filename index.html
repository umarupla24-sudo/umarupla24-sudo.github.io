The Problem with Most Doors

Why vinyl, wood, and steel doors get replaced

Most patio and entry doors are built from materials that perform well on installation day and degrade on a predictable schedule. Vinyl expands and contracts with temperature, loosening seals and warping frames over time. Wood absorbs moisture, swells, cracks, and demands a painting cycle to keep it functioning. Steel rusts at the edges, dents under impact, and conducts cold in northern climates. Every one of these failure modes is a cost you absorb — in maintenance time, in contractor visits, in eventual replacement.

The buyer who has already replaced a door once is the buyer who starts asking different questions the second time around.

The Composite Solution

Engineered to hold its form for the life of the home

MP Doors composite construction — engineered with HydroShield Technology — addresses every one of those failure modes by starting with a different material. Composite does not absorb water. It does not change dimension with temperature. It does not rust. It does not require a paint cycle. The frame you install is the frame you will have in year fifteen, without intervention.

That is not a warranty claim. It is a material property. And at a price point that starts at $798 — below some vinyl options on the same Home Depot shelf — it is also a discovery most buyers do not expect to make.
