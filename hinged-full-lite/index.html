Why This Door Performs

Composite Frame, All Six Sides

Composite Frame, All Six Sides

The frame, panels, and every edge are engineered with HydroShield Technology — a full-composite, 100% waterproof construction system applied to all six sides of the door.

Which means:

Moisture has no entry point — no corner to rot, no edge to split, no surface to delaminate — so the door you install today looks and operates the same in year fifteen as it does in year one.

Both Panels Operable

Both Panels Operable

Both the main active panel and the secondary panel open fully — with no center post dividing the opening.

Which means:

When you need the full width of the opening — moving furniture, bringing in a sofa, opening the room to a deck in summer — nothing is in your way.

Four Sizes, One Product Line

Four Sizes, One Product Line

Available in single door (30×80 and 36×80) and double door (60×80 and 72×80) configurations.

Which means:

Whether you are replacing a single back door or opening a living room to a patio, one product family covers the full range without custom ordering.

Weather-Stripping and Draining System

Weather-Stripping and Draining System

The door arrives double-bored and includes a patented water-resistant draining system with weather-stripping insulation factory-installed.

Which means:

The door is sealed and ready from day one — water that reaches the threshold drains away rather than pooling against the frame.

Energy Star — Every Zone

Energy Star — Every Zone

Energy Star certified across all four U.S. climate zones: Northern, North Central, South Central, and Southern.

Which means:

The thermal performance is documented and certified for every regional climate you might be buying this door for.

Exterior White — Paintable Finish

Exterior White — Paintable Finish

The factory finish is Smooth White — a neutral exterior tone that accepts paint if your home's color scheme calls for something else.

Which means:

You are not locked into a color — the composite surface accepts exterior paint and holds it without the surface preparation cycles other door materials require.

Built for Where You Live

Energy Star qualification in every U.S. climate zone means this door meets federal thermal performance standards from the Minnesota border to the Gulf Coast. The composite construction holds its geometry through temperature cycling and seasonal moisture exposure — which is why buyers in both Northern and Southern zones consistently report that the door operates the same in winter as it did on installation day.

In coastal climates, the composite frame resists the humidity and salt-air exposure that accelerates deterioration in painted frame materials over time. This is not a door engineered for one climate. It is a door engineered for all of them.

Limited Lifetime on structural components

Warranty

The structural components that cost the most to replace — frame, panels, and astragals — carry a Limited Lifetime Warranty for the original purchaser, valid as long as you own your home. The glass seal is covered for 20 years prorated, and the locking mechanism is covered for 10 years. The factory painted finish carries a 2-year warranty.

Labor costs are not included in the warranty, and installation by a licensed and bonded contractor is required for warranty coverage to apply. If your door arrives with visible damage, inspect it and report within 5 calendar days of delivery — call 1-888-366-7717 or email customerservice@mpdoors.com, and you will not be asked to return a 200-pound door to a retail store.
