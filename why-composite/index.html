What is composite construction for a patio door?

Composite is a material engineered for the conditions a patio door lives in every day: moisture, temperature swings, UV exposure, and constant mechanical use. It does not absorb water, change dimension with temperature, or degrade at the surface under sustained sun exposure.

For a homeowner in a humid climate, that means a frame that stays sealed and dimensionally stable through every wet season. In a cold climate, a frame that holds its geometry through freeze-thaw cycles without opening gaps in the weather seal. On a coast, a surface that resists corrosion and salt air without a maintenance cycle to keep it there.

The construction applies to all six sides of the door system — frame, panels, and every edge — engineered with HydroShield Technology so the protection is complete, not surface-level.

Frame Dimensional Stability

A composite frame holds its original geometry through heat, cold, and humidity. Hardware stays aligned with the strike. Seals maintain their compression. The door that operates smoothly on installation day operates the same way in year ten — because the frame it hangs in has not moved.

Sustained Seal Performance

Seal integrity depends on a frame that does not shift. When the frame holds its shape and the glass unit holds its position, the weather seal maintains consistent contact and compression — the condition that keeps air and water outside where they belong.

Surface Integrity

The composite surface resists UV degradation and holds its finish without the chalking, peeling, or refinishing cycle that other door materials run on in climates with sustained sun and temperature exposure.
