Modern Full Lite
Entry Door

MP Doors Modern Full Lite Entry Door

Four large glass panels. Clean lines. Nothing extra.

For buyers who want a contemporary glass entry that makes an architectural statement without ornamental detail — four substantial glass lites in a minimal composite frame, built for transitional and modern home exteriors.

Contemporary home design has a specific relationship with its entry door — the front door is not decorative in the traditional sense; it is architectural. The Modern Full Lite entry door is built for that relationship: four large glass panels in a clean, minimal frame that lets the glass and the proportions do the visual work without grilles, applied moulding, or decorative glass treatment. The composite frame underneath holds that design intention in place year after year, without the maintenance cycle that wood and painted steel entry doors run on.

Why This Door

Four Large Glass Lites — Minimal Frame

Four substantial glass panels set in a clean, minimal frame — no applied moulding, no grilles, no decorative treatment.

Which means the visual statement is made by proportion and glass area, not ornamentation — a design logic that reads as contemporary architecture rather than builder-grade upgrade.

LowE Clear and Frosted Glass Options

Available in LowE clear glass for maximum light transmission, or frosted for diffused light with privacy.

Which means buyers in open neighborhood positions who want privacy at the front entry have a stock frosted option — without specifying a custom glass treatment.

Composite Frame — Low Maintenance

The fiberglass door system with composite frame construction is 100% waterproof and does not rot, warp, split, delaminate, dent, or rust.

Which means the clean contemporary aesthetic you install on day one requires no maintenance cycle to maintain — the frame holds its finish and form without intervention.

Factory Pre-Hung System — Consistent Tolerances

Arrives as a factory-assembled, pre-hung system with factory-set clearances between slab and frame.

Which means the glass sits flat and the door operates precisely on installation day, without field adjustments that can introduce the misalignments that show most clearly in a glass-dominant door.

Energy Star — All Four Zones

Energy Star certified in all four U.S. climate zones — Northern, North Central, South Central, and Southern.

Which means the thermal performance of this glass-forward entry door is certified and documented for every regional climate.

Built for Where You Live

The four-lite glass configuration makes thermal performance of the glass unit directly relevant to every climate this door is installed in. Energy Star certification in all four U.S. climate zones means the glass and frame assembly meets federal standards in cold northern climates as well as hot southern ones.

The composite frame does not absorb moisture in northern winters — a condition that causes wood frames to swell against glass panels, creating pressure on the glazing and opening gaps in the weather seal. In southern and coastal climates, the composite surface does not accumulate the UV degradation and surface oxidation that requires periodic refinishing on painted steel and wood entry frames.
